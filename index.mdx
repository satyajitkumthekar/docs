Fitness is deadly simple.

At its core, getting fit just means doing a stack of habits on repeat, each with a set frequency. A workout, a high protein meal, a walk. All of it is just a habit you have to do repeatedly at the right frequency, and there is a fixed outcome, which is that you move closer to the peak state of your existence.

Now there are parts to mastering each of these habits.

1. Knowing what to do, how to do it, and when to do it.
2. Actually doing it.

The first aspect is purely knowledge or planning. It can be solved with sheer information.

The second part is purely mental. It is the ability to do what you know you have to do, consistently. And this is where fitness gets all the hype. Because in building the skill to consistently do what you're supposed to do, you have to solve a long tail of internal knots and beliefs. These are the same mental knots and beliefs that act as the base skills to become successful in any domain of life. You might have heard the terms. Discipline. Mindset. Yada yada.

Which means when you solve them, you become a person who is capable of doing these habits consistently. And it is why fitness goes beyond elevating your body and mind. It elevates the life you are living.
